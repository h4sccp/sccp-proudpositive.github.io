Welcome to San Carlos City Social Hygiene Unit

We are dedicated to promoting health, preventing the spread of sexually transmitted infections (STIs), including HIV/AIDS, and providing compassionate support to our community. Our commitment is to foster a stigma-free environment where everyone feels empowered to seek information, testing, and care.

Explore our website to learn more about our vision, mission, ongoing initiatives, and how you can access vital services. Together, we can build a healthier and more informed San Carlos City.

About Us

Our Vision

A healthy and empowered San Carlos City where every individual has access to comprehensive social hygiene services, fostering a community free from the burden of STIs and HIV/AIDS.

Our Mission

To deliver accessible, confidential, and high-quality social hygiene services, including prevention, testing, treatment, and counseling, through dedicated healthcare professionals and community partnerships, ensuring the well-being of all San Carlos City residents.

Our Team

Dr. Natasha Mae Benitez, Program Manager of Communicable Diseases and Chief of Health Informatics, leads innovative programs for diseases like tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS, STIs, leprosy, and WASH, while advancing data-driven healthcare and health equity. Ms. Caroline De Guzman, AIDS Nurse Coordinator, brings over 10 years as an HIV counselor and LGBTQ advocate. Ramjay Perci Novilla, Administrative Officer II, promotes innovation and inclusive leadership. Dr. Susan Benitez, City Health Officer II, oversees all health services with decades of rural experience, ensuring efficient delivery and community health awareness.

Our Values

  • Compassion: Providing care with empathy and understanding.
  • Confidentiality: Ensuring privacy and trust in all interactions.
  • Integrity: Upholding the highest ethical standards.
  • Accessibility: Making services available to everyone, regardless of background.
  • Empowerment: Educating and enabling individuals to make informed health decisions.

Our Initiatives

Proud and Positive Campaign (2024-Present)

Pioneered the first Pride Parade along the Minor Basilica’s pathway, with a ribbon formation, candle lighting, and an HIV speaker, reducing stigma. Led a color fun run, artistic competitions, and an HIV video with 7.9M views, growing our online platform to 35,000 followers and increasing HIV screenings from 456 in 2024 to 353 in Q1 2025.

Lakbay Kalusugan (launched July 2)

Ongoing free HIV and mental health education and screenings in barangays and schools (78 screened so far).

Pride Parade and HOPE Ambassadors (August)

Crowning youth pairs as HIV advocates in a public awareness campaign.

PHAISEZ (September)

A self-esteem and alternative livelihood initiative for women in the sex industry, offering free manicure/pedicure services and health talks.

Parol ng Pag-asa (December)

A World AIDS Day community lantern-making event to reduce stigma and increase awareness.
